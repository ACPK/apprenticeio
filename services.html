We make web apps for startups
and make developers more productive.

In our consulting practice, we design and develop web apps for our clients, who are usually startups.
That work has led us to create SaaS web apps for developers and web design and development training courses.

Web Design & Development

We are a full-service, never-subcontract team of 11 web developers, 5 web designers, and 1 energetic assistant. We have focused only on Ruby on Rails web applications since before Rails 1.0 was released in 2005.

Our process is to design the user interface first, then implement user stories. We work in weekly iterations and deploy to production often. We believe our process maximumizes product quality and flexibility of scope right up until launch.

Learn more about our process in our client handbook.

Workshops

We have an advantage in our consulting business of working on many web apps. We share what we've learned in our workshops, where we've trained hundreds of designers and developers from Harvard University, The New York Times, Raytheon, Mitre and more.

To learn more, email our workshop team at workshops@thoughtbot.com.

Kick Start

We provide an extra service called "Kick Start" for startups who want it. We interview and make recommendations on candidates for the company's first full-time designers or developers (often the CTO). This is often a challenge for non-technical founders and we're happy to help. We know many people in the community and have interviewed countless people over the years.

Typically, a kick start engagement will occur after a normal design and development engagement for a client, but we are open to doing placement only for the right businesses.

Our products
Hoptoad

The original “app error app.” The service collects errors generated by web apps, and aggregates the results. It has helped thousands of developers at companies like Kickstarter, Gowalla, and Localytics fix millions of errors.

Copycopter

Let your clients do the copy writing. Copycopter lets you easily add copy through the natural deploy cycle, and then make tweaks on live apps without requiring a deploy.
